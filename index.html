Support for Decision Making in Autonomous Vehicle

This project explored the possibility of increasing the automation level of Embraer's air vehicle systems, seeking proximity to their autonomy. The possibility of using a decision system based on the OODA cycle (Observe – Orient – ​​Decide – Act) was studied. This cycle was validated through drones, which were exposed to events that tested their efficiency. The project's final objective was to validate the OODA cycle in the proposed scenario, which consisted on executing a mission: filming a previously delimited region and determining the presence of objects of interest while on autonomous mode. The independent system had to decide whether to continue completing the task or return to base, based on monitoring information such as battery level, GPS position, and local wind. Initially, a simplified scenario was adopted, in which the drone lost communication with the controller and returned to the beginning of its trajectory efficiently (video). After this recognition and study phase, the project's final objective was implemented.

Computer Graphics

The goal of this project was to apply the knowledge and techniques acquired in the Computer Graphics module from the University of Porto. For this project, a four-wheeled motor vehicle and the terrain where the car travels were modeled and a graphical interface (GUI) was created with some controls to change scene parameters at runtime. Finally, an articulated crane with a magnet that interacts with the vehicle was modeled.

Numerical Propagation of Space Bodies' Orbits Around The Earth

To follow the growth of space study, Insper created a study to develop a software for analyzing orbital data. The project aimed to create a GUI in Matlab to simulate objects in orbit around the Earth and Moon. The model object of study contemplates the perturbations characteristic of the problem, such as atmospheric drag and gravitational force of other celestial bodies. Simulation involves the numerical integration of differential equations that model the trajectory of objects in orbit.

GAB - My computer language

For Insper's Computer Logic module's final project, I had to create my high-productivity programming language. This language contains variables, functions, conditionals, loops, and I/O. It is a language with reserved words in Portuguese, can print the value of mathematical expressions involving variables, and accepts inputs to assign values to variables. It also allows defining, calling, and assigning functions to variables. It has conditions like 'if' and 'else', and its mathematical expressions allow integers only.

Web Pages Parser In Cluster

For Insper's Supercomputers module's second project, I worked with a problem in which concurrency plays a fundamental role in achieving good performance: downloading and analyzing web pages. For this study, a crawler that identifies product pages on an e-commerce site and extracts basic product information was created. Given a product display page, the web crawler extracts the following information: product name, product description, product photo url, spot price, installment price, product category, and display page url. The identification of product pages is made based on their category, which means that the developed crawler is pointed to a page with the products' category and manages to obtain the product pages, dealing with possible listing pagination.
